SECTION 27-2029: HEAT AND HOT WATER REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

Under New York City Administrative Code §27-2029, all residential buildings in
New York City must provide adequate heat and hot water to tenants. This law
applies to all multiple dwellings and private dwellings in which the owner does
not reside.

HEAT REQUIREMENTS - Heating Season (October 1 through May 31):
- Between 6:00 AM and 10:00 PM: If the outside temperature falls below 55°F
  (12.8°C), the indoor temperature in every habitable room must be at least
  68°F (20°C).
- Between 10:00 PM and 6:00 AM: The indoor temperature must be maintained at
  a minimum of 62°F (16.7°C) regardless of the outside temperature.

HOT WATER REQUIREMENTS (Year-Round, 24 hours/day):
- Hot water must be maintained at a constant minimum temperature of 120°F
  (48.9°C) at the tap.
- Hot water must be available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year.
- Failure to provide hot water is a Class C immediately hazardous violation.

PENALTIES FOR HEAT/HOT WATER VIOLATIONS:
- Class C (Immediately Hazardous) violation: Must be corrected within 24 hours
- Civil penalty: $250–$500 per day for first violation
- Second violation within two years: $500–$1,000 per day
- Third or subsequent: $1,000–$2,500 per day
- Enhanced penalties during cold weather emergencies (below 28°F outdoor temp)

HPD EMERGENCY REPAIR PROGRAM:
If your landlord fails to restore heat within 24 hours of an HPD violation,
HPD may arrange emergency repairs and bill the landlord, placing a lien on the
property if unpaid.

WARRANTY OF HABITABILITY — New York Real Property Law §235-b
================================================================================

Every residential lease in New York State contains an implied warranty of
habitability. This statutory warranty cannot be waived by the tenant, even in
the lease. Under RPL §235-b, landlords must:

1. Maintain the premises in a habitable condition fit for human occupation.
2. Maintain all building services, facilities, and equipment in good repair.
3. Provide heat and hot water as required by the Housing Maintenance Code.
4. Keep the building free from vermin, cockroaches, rodents, and pests.
5. Maintain all electrical, plumbing, sanitary, heating, and ventilation systems.
6. Keep all exterior and interior public areas in a safe and sanitary condition.
7. Comply with all applicable building, housing, and health codes.

The warranty of habitability applies to:
- All residential leases (month-to-month and term leases)
- All apartment types (rent-stabilized, market-rate, rent-controlled)
- All dwelling sizes (studio apartments to single-family homes)

The warranty CANNOT be:
- Waived in the lease agreement
- Bargained away by the tenant
- Disclaimed by the landlord

TENANT REMEDIES UNDER WARRANTY OF HABITABILITY:
1. Withhold or reduce rent (following proper procedure)
2. Repair and deduct (minor repairs up to $300 per RPL §235-a)
3. File an HP action in Housing Court for inspections and orders to repair
4. Counterclaim in non-payment eviction proceedings
5. Sue for damages in Civil Court or Housing Court
6. Constructive eviction (abandon unit and terminate lease)

RENT WITHHOLDING PROCEDURE — RPAPL §755
================================================================================

New York Real Property Actions and Proceedings Law §755 provides that a tenant
may withhold rent when there is a "rent impairing violation" or condition that
materially affects habitability. IMPORTANT: Tenants cannot simply stop paying.

STEPS REQUIRED BEFORE WITHHOLDING RENT:
1. Notify the landlord in WRITING of the dangerous or hazardous condition.
2. The notice must describe the condition with specificity (what, where, when).
3. Allow the landlord reasonable time to repair:
   - Heat/hot water: 24-48 hours (life safety emergency)
   - Other conditions: typically 30 days
4. If landlord fails to repair: Begin placing withheld rent in a separate
   bank account (escrow). Keep records of all deposits.
5. Continue depositing rent amounts each month into the escrow account.

WHAT HAPPENS IN COURT:
If your landlord sues you for non-payment, you can assert the warranty of
habitability as a defense and counterclaim. The court will:
- Order an inspection of the premises
- Determine the percentage by which habitability is impaired
- Reduce your rent obligation by that percentage
- Order the landlord to make repairs

CONSTRUCTIVE EVICTION:
If conditions are so severe that the apartment is uninhabitable and you are
forced to move out, you may claim constructive eviction:
- Terminate the lease without liability
- Stop paying rent from the date you vacate
- Sue for damages: moving costs, difference in rent at new apartment, etc.
- File a claim for personal injury damages if applicable

REPAIR AND DEDUCT — New York Real Property Law §235-a
================================================================================

For minor repairs, tenants may:
1. Notify the landlord in writing of the needed repair.
2. Allow the landlord 30 days to make the repair (less for emergencies).
3. If landlord fails: Hire a licensed contractor to make the repair.
4. Deduct the cost from the next month's rent (maximum: $300).
5. Retain all receipts and notify landlord of deduction in writing.

This remedy is for MINOR repairs only. Major structural issues require the
full warranty of habitability process or court action.

HPD COMPLAINT PROCESS — NYC Housing Preservation and Development
================================================================================

HPD is the city agency that enforces the Housing Maintenance Code and Multiple
Dwelling Law. HPD inspects for code violations and issues violation notices.

HOW TO FILE A COMPLAINT:
1. Call 311 (available 24/7, multilingual service)
   - Say: "Heat complaint" or "Housing code violation"
   - Available in over 180 languages
2. Online: nyc.gov/hpd → "Report a Problem"
3. NYC311 mobile app
4. In-person: HPD offices at 100 Gold Street, Manhattan (Mon-Fri 8am-5pm)

WHAT HAPPENS AFTER FILING:
- You receive a complaint number for tracking
- HPD schedules an inspection:
  * Emergency conditions (heat, gas, structural): Within 24 hours
  * Hazardous conditions: Within a few days
  * Non-emergency: Within 45 days
- If violation found: Notice of Violation (NOV) served on landlord
- Landlord must certify correction within the time frame specified
- If uncertified: HPD may reinspect and impose additional penalties

EMERGENCY HEAT COMPLAINT (October 1 - May 31):
- Automatically treated as Class C (Immediately Hazardous)
- HPD will attempt to inspect within 24 hours
- Landlord must restore heat within 24 hours of the NOV
- If heat not restored: HPD Emergency Repair Program may intervene

311 COMPLAINT TRACKING:
- Track your complaint at 311.nyc.gov
- Sign up for text or email updates
- Complaint status available 24/7 online

NYC HOUSING MAINTENANCE CODE — VIOLATION CLASSIFICATIONS
================================================================================

CLASS A — NON-HAZARDOUS:
Minor conditions not immediately dangerous to health, safety, or life.
Examples: Missing light in common area, cracked tile, worn carpet
Correction time: 90 days

CLASS B — HAZARDOUS:
Conditions hazardous to life, health, or safety of residents.
Examples: Mold, rodents, vermin infestation, inadequate lighting in stairwells
Correction time: 30 days

CLASS C — IMMEDIATELY HAZARDOUS:
Conditions immediately dangerous to life, health, or safety.
Examples: NO HEAT or HOT WATER, lack of fire exits, gas leaks, lead paint
           hazards (with children under 6), broken elevator
Correction time: 24 hours (immediate action required)

IMPORTANT: Heat and hot water violations are ALWAYS Class C.

CHECKING VIOLATIONS ON YOUR BUILDING:
- HPD Building Information System: a810-bisweb.nyc.gov/biswebapp/
- Enter your address to see all open violations
- This is public information and useful evidence in court

TENANT PROTECTION AGAINST RETALIATION — Real Property Law §223-b
================================================================================

Your landlord CANNOT retaliate against you for exercising your legal rights.
Protected activities include:
- Filing a complaint with HPD, 311, or any government agency
- Joining or organizing a tenant association
- Participating in a rent strike (if properly organized)
- Testifying or providing information to authorities
- Exercising any rights under the lease or law

PROHIBITED RETALIATORY ACTIONS:
- Eviction or filing an eviction proceeding
- Substantial alteration of the lease terms
- Failure to renew a lease
- Interruption or reduction of services
- Harassment or intimidation
- Bringing code enforcement proceedings against tenant

PRESUMPTION OF RETALIATION:
If the landlord takes any adverse action within 90 days of your protected
activity, the LAW PRESUMES the action is retaliatory. The burden then shifts
to the landlord to prove the action was NOT retaliatory (very difficult).

REMEDIES FOR RETALIATION:
- Injunctive relief (court order stopping the retaliation)
- Actual damages
- Punitive damages
- Attorney's fees
- Defense to eviction proceedings

NYC RENT STABILIZATION LAW
================================================================================

COVERAGE: Buildings built before 1974 with 6 or more residential units,
and certain buildings constructed with J-51 or 421-a tax benefits.

PROTECTIONS FOR RENT-STABILIZED TENANTS:
1. Rent can only be increased by percentages set by the NYC Rent Guidelines Board
   (RGB) each year (announced in June each year).
2. Right to a renewal lease on the same terms and conditions.
3. Protection from eviction without legally specified "good cause."
4. Right to have immediate family members succeed to the lease.

GOOD CAUSE FOR EVICTION (Rent-Stabilized):
Landlord may only evict for: non-payment of rent; breach of lease; illegal
use of apartment; nuisance; landlord wants apartment for primary personal use;
building demolition (with conditions).

RENT REDUCTION ORDERS:
If your landlord fails to provide heat, hot water, or other required services,
you may file a complaint with the Division of Housing and Community Renewal
(DHCR). DHCR can issue a Rent Reduction Order, reducing your legal regulated
rent until services are restored.

DHCR Contact: hcr.ny.gov | (718) 739-6400 | 25 Beaver St, Manhattan

HOUSING COURT AND LEGAL PROCESS
================================================================================

NYC HOUSING COURT:
- Manhattan: 111 Centre Street, New York, NY 10013
- Bronx: 851 Grand Concourse, Bronx, NY 10451
- Brooklyn: 141 Livingston Street, Brooklyn, NY 11201
- Queens: 89-17 Sutphin Blvd, Jamaica, NY 11435
- Staten Island: 927 Castleton Avenue, Staten Island, NY 10310

HP ACTIONS (Housing Part Petitions):
Tenants can file HP Actions in Housing Court to compel repairs:
1. File HP petition at Housing Court clerk's window (free to file)
2. Court orders an inspection by HPD
3. If violations found: Judge can order repairs with a deadline
4. If landlord fails: Held in contempt (fines, jail)

HP Actions are particularly effective for emergency conditions like no heat.

LAWYERS FOR THE DAY PROGRAM:
Free legal assistance available at Housing Court on walk-in basis.
Contact Housing Court Answers: (212) 962-4795

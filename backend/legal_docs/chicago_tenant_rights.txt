CHICAGO RESIDENTIAL LANDLORD AND TENANT ORDINANCE (RLTO)
Chicago Municipal Code Chapter 5-12
Source: City of Chicago Department of Housing
Last Updated: 2024

================================================================================
OVERVIEW AND SCOPE
================================================================================

The Chicago Residential Landlord and Tenant Ordinance (RLTO), codified at
Chicago Municipal Code Chapter 5-12, is one of the strongest tenant protection
laws in the United States. It was enacted in 1986 and has been strengthened
multiple times since.

COVERAGE — RLTO APPLIES TO:
- Most rental residential properties within City of Chicago limits
- Buildings with any number of units (with limited exceptions below)

EXCEPTIONS — RLTO DOES NOT APPLY TO:
- Owner-occupied buildings with 6 or fewer units (most provisions)
- Units in hotels, motels, rooming houses (unless occupied for 32+ days)
- School dormitories
- Public housing managed by Chicago Housing Authority (CHA)
- Units occupied under purchase contracts

================================================================================
SECTION 5-12-110: TENANT'S REMEDIES FOR FAILURE TO MAINTAIN PREMISES
================================================================================

If your landlord materially fails to comply with the rental agreement or with
applicable building codes that materially affect health and safety, you have
the following statutory remedies:

PRECONDITION — WRITTEN NOTICE REQUIREMENT:
Before exercising most remedies, you MUST give written notice to the landlord.
The notice must:
1. State the address and unit number
2. Describe the condition requiring repair with reasonable specificity
3. State which remedy you intend to exercise if repairs are not made
4. Be delivered to the landlord by:
   - Personal delivery (get a signed receipt)
   - First class mail (effective upon mailing, but allow delivery time)
   - Email if landlord has previously agreed to email notices in writing

NOTICE PERIODS:
- Life-threatening emergencies (no heat in extreme cold): 24 hours notice
- Serious health/safety violations (heat below legal minimum): 14 days notice
- Standard code violations: 14 days notice
- Minor defects: 30 days notice

================================================================================
HEAT REQUIREMENTS — Chicago Municipal Code §13-196-410
================================================================================

MANDATORY MINIMUM TEMPERATURES:
- Heating Season: September 15 through June 1
- Required indoor temperature: Minimum 68°F (20°C) at all times during
  the heating season
- Measurement taken in all rooms used for living purposes
- The 68°F requirement applies 24 hours a day throughout heating season

HOT WATER REQUIREMENTS:
- Hot water must be provided at all times
- Minimum temperature: 120°F at the tap
- Water heater must be properly maintained and functional

CONSEQUENCES FOR HEAT VIOLATIONS:
- Building code violation subject to $500–$1,000 fine per day
- Tenant remedies (detailed below) activate after 14-day notice
- Severe cases may result in building being declared uninhabitable
- City may place building in receivership if landlord is non-responsive

================================================================================
REMEDY 1: REPAIR AND DEDUCT — §5-12-110(a)
================================================================================

After giving proper written notice and the landlord fails to repair within the
required time period:

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
- Hire a licensed contractor to fix the defect
- Deduct the cost from your next rent payment
- No court approval required in advance

LIMITS:
- Maximum deduction: The LESSER of $500 OR one-half (1/2) of your monthly rent
- Example 1: Monthly rent = $1,200 → Maximum = $500 (lesser)
- Example 2: Monthly rent = $800 → Maximum = $400 (1/2 of $800)
- Example 3: Monthly rent = $600 → Maximum = $300 (1/2 of $600)

REQUIREMENTS:
- Must use a licensed and bonded contractor (not do-it-yourself)
- Keep ALL receipts and contractor invoices
- Provide copies of receipts to landlord when deducting
- Attach written notice to rent payment explaining the deduction
- Only for repairs, not for recurring conditions requiring ongoing remedy

================================================================================
REMEDY 2: WITHHOLD RENT — §5-12-110(b)
================================================================================

After giving proper 14-day written notice and landlord fails to repair:

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
- Withhold from rent an amount that "reasonably reflects the diminution in value
  of the dwelling unit due to the material noncompliance"
- Must be proportional to the severity of the defect

IMPORTANT RULES:
1. You must have been current on rent at the time you sent the notice
2. You cannot withhold rent for conditions you or your guests caused
3. Keep detailed records of all communications with landlord
4. Save copies of all notices (photos of postmarked envelopes are useful)
5. Document the condition with photos, videos, and written records

RECOMMENDED (NOT REQUIRED): Place withheld amounts in a separate escrow account.
While the RLTO does not require escrow, keeping funds separate proves good faith
and protects you if the matter goes to court.

HOW MUCH TO WITHHOLD:
Courts look at the percentage by which habitability is impaired.
Typical amounts (not legally binding, used as guidance):
- No heat in winter: 50–100% rent withholding appropriate
- Serious infestation: 20–40%
- Minor code violations: 5–15%

================================================================================
REMEDY 3: TERMINATE LEASE — §5-12-110(c)
================================================================================

For conditions that pose an immediate danger to health or safety, or when
landlord fails to correct within the required notice period:

WHEN YOU CAN TERMINATE:
- Condition is so severe it poses immediate danger to health or safety, OR
- Material noncompliance with the lease or building code, and 14 days notice
  has expired without correction

HOW TO TERMINATE:
1. Send written notice stating you are terminating the lease
2. State the date of termination (must be at least 14 days after notice)
3. State the specific condition causing termination
4. Move out by the stated termination date

EFFECT OF TERMINATION:
- Released from all further rent obligations from move-out date
- May sue for: security deposit return, prepaid rent, moving costs, and
  damages related to the landlord's failure
- Landlord cannot charge you for early termination

================================================================================
REMEDY 4: SEEK COURT RELIEF — §5-12-110(d)
================================================================================

At any time (you do not need to use the other remedies first), you may file
suit in Circuit Court of Cook County to:
- Compel landlord to make specific repairs (injunction)
- Recover actual damages suffered due to the condition
- Recover attorneys' fees if you prevail
- In cases of willful landlord conduct, seek punitive damages

Small Claims: Claims up to $10,000 may be filed in Small Claims Court without
an attorney (filing fee approximately $75–$200 depending on claim amount).

================================================================================
SECTION 5-12-130: RETALIATION PROTECTIONS
================================================================================

PROTECTED ACTIVITIES:
Your landlord CANNOT retaliate against you for:
- Filing a complaint with any city or government agency (311, Building Dept.)
- Exercising any right under the RLTO
- Joining, organizing, or participating in a tenants' union or organization
- Testifying in any legal proceeding related to your tenancy
- Complaining to the landlord about conditions (written or verbal)

PROHIBITED RETALIATORY ACTIONS:
- Eviction or threat of eviction
- Rent increase (except already-scheduled increases)
- Reduction of services or amenities
- Refusal to renew the lease
- Interference with your quiet enjoyment

PRESUMPTION OF RETALIATION:
If the landlord takes adverse action within 12 MONTHS of protected activity,
the law PRESUMES the action is retaliatory. The landlord must prove the
action had a legitimate, non-retaliatory basis.

REMEDY FOR RETALIATION:
Greater of:
(a) Two months' rent, OR
(b) Actual damages suffered
PLUS attorneys' fees and costs in either case.

================================================================================
SECURITY DEPOSIT PROTECTIONS — §5-12-080 through §5-12-082
================================================================================

HOLDING REQUIREMENTS:
- Must be held in a federally insured interest-bearing savings account
- Must be kept separate from landlord's personal or business funds
- Landlord must provide written receipt within 2 business days of receipt
- Receipt must include: bank name, account number, interest rate

INTEREST:
- Tenant entitled to interest on security deposit held more than 6 months
- Interest at the rate paid by the bank (must disclose the rate)
- Interest must be returned with deposit or paid annually

RETURN OF DEPOSIT:
- Must return within 30 days of lease termination if no deductions
- Must return within 45 days if itemizing deductions (with receipts)
- Itemized list of deductions required in writing

PENALTY FOR NON-COMPLIANCE:
If landlord fails to comply with security deposit rules:
- Tenant entitled to TWICE the security deposit amount as damages
- Plus attorneys' fees and costs

================================================================================
REQUIRED LANDLORD DISCLOSURES — §5-12-100
================================================================================

Chicago landlords must disclose to tenants at lease signing:
1. Summary of Chicago RLTO rights (city-published document)
2. Any pending building code violations on record with the City
3. Known mold conditions in the unit
4. Whether the building is in a flood plain
5. Name, address, and telephone number of the owner
6. Name, address, and telephone number of the property manager
7. Whether any common area utilities are included in rent and how allocated

FAILURE TO DISCLOSE:
If landlord fails to provide required disclosures, tenant may terminate the
lease within 30 days of signing and recover one month's rent in damages.

================================================================================
ILLEGAL LOCKOUT AND SELF-HELP EVICTION — §5-12-160
================================================================================

It is ILLEGAL for a landlord to evict you without a court order.
Prohibited actions include:
- Changing the locks
- Removing or damaging your personal property
- Removing doors or windows
- Shutting off utilities (electricity, gas, water)
- Using threats, intimidation, or violence
- Removing your belongings from the unit

REMEDY FOR ILLEGAL LOCKOUT:
- Immediate: Call Chicago Police (312) 744-5000 or 911
- Civil remedy: Sue for actual damages PLUS two months' rent
- Court can also order immediate restoration of possession

EVICTION PROCESS IN CHICAGO (Legal Process):
1. Landlord gives proper written notice (5 days for non-payment, 10 days
   for lease violation, 30 days for month-to-month lease termination)
2. If tenant does not comply: Landlord files eviction lawsuit in Circuit Court
3. Court hearing scheduled
4. If landlord wins: Sheriff executes eviction (no sooner than 5 days after order)
NEVER leave just because a landlord tells you to. Wait for a court order.

FILING A COMPLAINT WITH THE CITY OF CHICAGO
================================================================================

311 SERVICE REQUESTS:
- Call: 311 (from Chicago) or (312) 744-5000 (outside Chicago)
- Online: 311.chicago.gov
- App: Chicago 311 mobile app
- Available 24/7; multilingual service available

COMPLAINT TYPES TO FILE:
- "Heat Complaint" — for heating season violations
- "Building Code Violation" — for code violations
- "Rodent/Pest Complaint" — for infestation
- "Illegal Dumping" — for unsafe conditions on property

DEPARTMENT OF BUILDINGS:
- (312) 744-5000
- chicago.gov/dob
- Conducts inspections for building code violations
- Issues citations and fines to non-compliant landlords

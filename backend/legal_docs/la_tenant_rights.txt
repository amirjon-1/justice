================================================================================
CALIFORNIA HABITABILITY STANDARDS — Civil Code §1941 and §1941.1
================================================================================

California law requires landlords to maintain rental properties in a "habitable"
condition at all times. California Civil Code §1941 places this duty on the
landlord regardless of what the lease says.

A DWELLING IS HABITABLE UNDER §1941.1 WHEN IT HAS:
1. Effective waterproofing and weather protection of roof and exterior walls,
   including unbroken windows and doors
2. Plumbing or gas facilities maintained in good working order (conforming to
   law at time of installation)
3. A water supply approved under applicable law providing hot and cold running
   water connected to a sewage disposal system
4. Heating facilities in good working order (conforming to law at time of install)
5. Electrical lighting, wiring, and equipment in good working order
6. Building, grounds, and appurtenances clean, sanitary, and free from debris,
   filth, rubbish, garbage, rodents, and vermin
7. Adequate garbage and rubbish storage and removal facilities
8. Floors, stairways, and railings maintained in good repair
9. Locks on: main entrance door, deadbolt on entry doors (30-inch grip), window
   locks on ground floor windows

HOT WATER REQUIREMENTS (California):
- Landlords must provide hot water sufficient for ordinary use
- LA requires water temperature of 120°F minimum
- Failure to provide hot water violates California habitability law

HEATING REQUIREMENTS:
- Heating facilities must be present and working
- LA Municipal Code requires heating capable of maintaining 70°F in habitable rooms
- Failure to maintain working heat violates both state and city law

TENANT REMEDIES UNDER CALIFORNIA LAW — Civil Code §1942 et seq.
================================================================================

REMEDY 1: REPAIR AND DEDUCT — Civil Code §1942
After giving landlord reasonable time to repair (usually 30 days; less for
emergencies posing immediate health/safety risk):

- You may cause the repair to be made
- Deduct the cost from rent
- Maximum: One month's rent per incident
- May only use this remedy TWICE in any 12-month period
- Must use a licensed contractor
- Keep all receipts

Example: Monthly rent = $2,000. Maximum repair and deduct = $2,000.
(LA's cap is higher than many other cities due to high rents)

REMEDY 2: RENT WITHHOLDING — Civil Code §1942 (implied)
You may withhold rent when the landlord breaches the warranty of habitability.
IMPORTANT: Courts are strict about this. Required steps:
1. Notify landlord in writing of the condition
2. Allow reasonable time to repair
3. Document everything (photos, videos, written communications)
4. Continue to deposit rent in a separate account
5. Be prepared to prove habitability breach in court

REMEDY 3: ABANDONMENT / CONSTRUCTIVE EVICTION
If conditions are so severe that the apartment is essentially uninhabitable:
- You may vacate and terminate the lease
- Must document conditions thoroughly before leaving
- May sue for damages: moving costs, rent differential, personal injury
- Must give landlord written notice of intent to terminate

REMEDY 4: REDUCTION IN SERVICES PETITION (Rent-Stabilized Only)
If your unit is covered by the LA Rent Stabilization Ordinance, you may
file a petition with LAHD for rent reduction due to reduction in services.

LOS ANGELES RENT STABILIZATION ORDINANCE (RSO) — LAMC Chapter XV, Art. 1
================================================================================

COVERAGE — RSO APPLIES TO:
- Rental units built on or before October 1, 1978 (or Certificate of Occupancy
  issued on or before that date)
- Units in Los Angeles city limits (not LA County or other cities)
- 2+ units on a parcel (condos excluded unless built before 1978 and rented)
- Applies to approximately 650,000 units in Los Angeles

RSO DOES NOT APPLY TO:
- Units built after October 1, 1978
- Single-family homes and condos (with limited exceptions per AB 1482)
- Units where landlord lives in adjacent unit in small building
- Hotels, motels, and similar transient accommodations

RSO ANNUAL RENT INCREASES:
- Landlord may raise rent by the annual allowable increase (set each year)
- 2024 allowable increase: 4% (varies by year, tied to CPI)
- No more than one rent increase per 12-month period
- 30 days advance written notice required for any rent increase
- Rent increases ABOVE the allowable amount are VOID and uncollectable

RSO JUST CAUSE EVICTION PROTECTIONS:
Under the RSO, landlords may only evict RSO-covered tenants for specific
"just cause" reasons:
TENANT-CAUSED JUST CAUSES:
1. Nonpayment of rent
2. Breach of lease provision (with opportunity to cure)
3. Maintaining a nuisance
4. Illegal use of unit
5. Denial of landlord's right to entry (after proper notice)
6. Subletting without permission

LANDLORD-CAUSED NO-FAULT EVICTIONS (require relocation assistance):
7. Owner or immediate family member occupancy
8. Demolition or removal from rental market (Ellis Act)
9. Substantial rehabilitation (limited)
10. Government order to vacate

RELOCATION ASSISTANCE REQUIREMENTS:
For no-fault evictions, landlords must pay relocation assistance:
- Small landlord: 1 month rent
- Large landlord (≥4 units): 3 months rent
- Elderly, disabled, or families with minor children: Additional 1 month rent
- All cases: City filing fee of $1,875 (paid to City, credited against assistance)

AB 1482 — STATEWIDE RENT CAP AND JUST CAUSE (Single-Family, Post-1978 Units):
California AB 1482 (effective Jan 1, 2020) provides protections for units
NOT covered by local rent control:
- Annual rent increase cap: 5% + local CPI, maximum 10%
- Just cause required for eviction after 12 months of tenancy
- Relocation assistance for no-fault evictions: 1 month's rent

================================================================================
LA HOUSING DEPARTMENT (LAHD) COMPLAINT PROCESS
================================================================================

LAHD is the City of Los Angeles agency that enforces the RSO and housing
habitability codes. LAHD handles:
- Rent control complaints
- Habitability complaints (health and safety violations)
- Illegal rent increase complaints
- Retaliatory eviction complaints
- Landlord failure to register unit (all RSO units must be registered)

HOW TO FILE A HABITABILITY COMPLAINT WITH LAHD:
1. Online: lahd.lacity.org → "File a Complaint"
2. By phone: (866) 557-7368 (LAHD hotline) or 311
3. In person: LAHD offices at 3550 Wilshire Blvd, Suite 1500, Los Angeles

WHAT HAPPENS AFTER FILING:
- Inspector scheduled (1-3 weeks for non-emergency; 24-48 hours for emergency)
- Inspector visits property, documents violations
- Notice to Comply issued to landlord (30 days to correct)
- Follow-up inspection scheduled
- If violations persist: Administrative citation ($200–$1,000+ per violation)
- Severe/persistent violations: Case referred to City Attorney for prosecution

LAHD RENT REDUCTION PETITION:
For RSO tenants: If your landlord reduces services or fails to maintain
habitability, file a Petition for Reduction in Services (on LAHD form).
Outcome: LAHD may reduce your legal maximum rent until violations are fixed.

LAHD RSO UNIT REGISTRATION:
- All RSO units must be registered with LAHD annually
- Landlord must pay registration fee
- Failure to register: Landlord cannot collect rent increases
- Check registration: lahd.lacity.org/residents

LOS ANGELES JUST CAUSE EVICTION ORDINANCE (LARSO Update — 2024)
================================================================================

Effective 2023, the Los Angeles City Council expanded Just Cause Eviction
protections. Key points:

EXTENDED TO NEWER UNITS: Just cause protections now apply after 12 months
of occupancy, including many units not covered by RSO (post-1978 buildings,
single-family homes — within City of LA limits).

PERMITTED JUST CAUSES:
- Non-payment of rent (after 3-day notice)
- Habitually late rent payment (3+ times in 12 months, each ≥5 days late)
- Damaging the unit
- Criminal activity on premises
- Nuisance
- Denial of landlord access (after proper notice per Civil Code §1954)
- Owner/family member move-in
- Withdrawal from rental market

TENANT PROTECTIONS FOR NO-FAULT EVICTIONS:
- 60-day minimum notice for tenants with 1+ year of tenancy
- Relocation assistance based on unit size:
  * Studio: $9,200
  * 1 bedroom: $12,600
  * 2 bedroom: $14,900
  * 3+ bedroom: $18,000
  (amounts subject to annual adjustment by LAHD)

ANTI-HARASSMENT ORDINANCE — LAMC §151.33
================================================================================

The LA Anti-Harassment Ordinance (passed 2021) prohibits landlords from
taking the following actions with intent to cause a tenant to vacate:
- Interrupting, terminating, or failing to provide housing services
- Failing to perform and complete timely repairs
- Removing amenities without consent
- Threatening to report tenants to immigration authorities
- Interfering with tenant's right to organize
- Entering a tenant's unit without proper notice and cause
- Verbal abuse, intimidation, or coercion
- Disrupting tenant's peaceful enjoyment of the premises

PENALTIES: Up to $15,000 per violation + actual damages + attorneys' fees.
Tenants may sue in Civil Court or file complaint with LAHD.

EVICTION PROCESS IN CALIFORNIA (Civil Code §1161 et seq.)
================================================================================

California's eviction process requires court involvement at every stage.
A landlord CANNOT evict you without a court judgment and sheriff enforcement.

STEP 1 — NOTICE:
- Non-payment: 3-day notice to pay or quit
- Lease violation: 3-day notice to cure or quit
- No-fault: 60-day notice (30 days if tenancy less than 1 year)

STEP 2 — UNLAWFUL DETAINER LAWSUIT:
If you do not comply with the notice, landlord may file an Unlawful Detainer
(UD) lawsuit in Los Angeles Superior Court (Stanley Mosk Courthouse or branch).

STEP 3 — COURT HEARING:
- You have 5 business days to respond to the UD summons
- Defenses include: improper notice, landlord breach of habitability,
  retaliation, discrimination, RSO violations, improper rent calculation
- If you assert habitability defense, court will schedule inspection

STEP 4 — JUDGMENT:
If landlord wins, court issues judgment. You have 5 days to vacate.
If you raise habitability defense and win, landlord pays your attorneys' fees.

STEP 5 — LOCKOUT:
Only the Los Angeles County Sheriff can enforce an eviction. Landlord
cannot change locks without a court-ordered writ of possession.

NEVER IGNORE A UD SUMMONS — You have only 5 business days to respond.
